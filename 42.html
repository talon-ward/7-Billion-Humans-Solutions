Size

   **| jmp01:
   **| jmp02:
   01| step NW, N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W
   02| if H != "a datacube" or
   **|    S == "a shredder"
   03| jump jmp02
   04| pickUp H
   05| "mem 1" = nearest "shredder"
   06| step "mem 1"
   07| "mem 1" = calc "my item" / "10"
   **| jmp03:
   08| if H < "mem 1"
   09|| step E
   10| if H > "mem 1"
   11|| step W
   12| if H == "mem 1"
   13|| giveTo S
   14|| jump jmp01
   15| jump jmp03

The size solution is fairly obvious. The speed solution primarily centers around replacing the slow "calc" operation with a fast binary "if" tree. The speed is variable due to random inputs; it may take multiple runs to get down to 140, and it may be possible to get even lower.
